Mission

BDWatch is a civil society–led watchdog dedicated to documenting human rights violations and threats to democracy in Bangladesh. We collect credible evidence, protect sources, and transform data into practical actions for accountability and reform.

What We Do

Monitor & Verify

Real-time tracking of incidents impacting life, liberty, assembly, speech, and press using open-source intelligence (OSINT), field reports, and partner submissions with rigorous multi-step verification.

Document & Preserve

Securely archiving testimonies, media, and geolocated evidence with standardized coding for trend analysis, ensuring survivor-centric protocols and trauma-informed methods.

Analyze & Report

Producing monthly briefs, in-depth reports, and dashboards for researchers and policymakers, with early-warning indicators for escalation and mass-atrocity risks.

Advocate & Engage

Submitting evidence to national and international bodies, offering policy recommendations, and training civil society on documentation and digital security.

Our Approach

Independence

No party or government affiliation. Transparent funding.

Rigor

Evidence standards aligned with Berkeley Protocol on Digital Open Source Investigations.

Safety

Confidential reporting and secure communications with do-no-harm principles.

Dignity

Survivor consent, anonymity by default, and respectful storytelling.

Bilingual Access

Content in English and Bangla to maximize accessibility.

Priority Focus Areas (2025)

  • Extrajudicial killings, enforced disappearances, custodial torture.
  • Suppression of peaceful assembly, association, and expression.
  • Attacks on journalists, academics, students, and minorities.
  • Election-related violence and misuse of security laws.
  • Digital repression, surveillance, and internet shutdowns.
