Mission

BDWatch is a civil society–led watchdog dedicated to documenting human rights violations and threats to democracy in Bangladesh. We collect credible evidence, protect sources, and transform data into practical actions for accountability and reform.

What We Do

Monitor & Verify

Real-time monitoring of incidents affecting life, liberty, assembly, speech, and press. Open-source intelligence (OSINT), field reporting, and partner submissions. Multi-step verification and chain-of-custody for digital evidence.

Document & Preserve

Secure archive of testimonies, media, legal records, and geolocated proof. Standardized incident coding for trend analysis and longitudinal research. Survivor-centric protocols and trauma-informed interviewing.

Analyze & Report

Monthly situation briefs and deep-dive reports on patterns and perpetrators. Datasets and dashboards for researchers, journalists, and policymakers. Early-warning indicators for escalation and mass-atrocity risk.

Advocate & Engage

Submissions to national bodies and international mechanisms. Policy recommendations aligned with international human rights law. Training for civil society on documentation, safety, and digital hygiene.

Our Approach

Independence

No party or government affiliation. Funding transparency.

Rigor

Evidence standards aligned with Berkeley Protocol on Digital Open Source Investigations and best-practice chain-of-custody.

Safety

Confidential reporting, secure communications, and do-no-harm principles.

Dignity

Survivor consent, anonymity by default, and respectful storytelling.

Bilingual Access

Outputs in English and Bangla to maximize reach.

Priority Focus Areas (2025)

  • Extrajudicial killings, enforced disappearances, custodial torture.
  • Suppression of peaceful assembly, association, and expression.
  • Attacks on journalists, academics, students, and minorities.
  • Election-related violence and misuse of security laws.
  • Digital repression, surveillance, and internet shutdowns.
